The font's genesis traces back to Cachin's immersion in the world of Bobst Graphic, 1972–1981 (2019), where she stumbled upon captivating images of CRT screens [1]. These screens, pivotal in controlling text input on the Eurocat phototypesetting apparatus, featured a distinctive matrix typeface characterized by a visible construction grid. Notably, the dots composing the letters intricately melded into the background grid, forming captivating yet unconventional configurations. It was this organic fusion, this delightful glitch within the structured grid, that served as the fertile ground for Eurocat's inception. During a residency organized by Maxitype in the summer of 2022, Cachin meticulously crafted the foundational alphabet of Eurocat. Subsequently, she expanded the font family, ensuring coherence across its various weights — thin, light, regular, medium, and bold. Notably, the essence of the dot matrix modules, intertwined with the backdrop grid, evolves seamlessly across each weight, a feature seamlessly preserved within the variable format font.
